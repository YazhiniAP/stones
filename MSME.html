MSME Registration

Micro, Small and Medium Enterprises (MSME) sector has emerged as a highly vibrant and dynamic sector of the Indian economy over the last five decades. Ministry of Micro, Small & Medium Enterprises promotes growth and development of the MSME Sector, by providing support to existing enterprises and encouraging creation of new enterprises. To avail all these beneficial schemes, one needs to do the MSME registration. MSME registration is also known as Udyog Aadhaar or SSI registration.

Micro Enterprises: Investment in plant and machinery is not more than ₹1 crore and the annual turnover is not more than ₹5 crores.
Small Enterprises: Investment in plant and machinery is not more than ₹10 crores and the annual turnover is not more than ₹50 crores.
Medium Enterprises: Investment in plant and machinery is not more than ₹50 crores and the annual turnover is not more than ₹250 crores.

Some common questions
were often asked

Who is eligible for MSME?

To be eligible for registering under MSME, the business should fall under the investment or turnover limits that the government has established. The type of organisation doesn’t matter and you can be an LLP, one-person company, Hindu joint family, etc.

What is the validity period of the MSME certificate?

MSME certificates are valid as long as the enterprises are up and functioning. On the other hand, if you are getting a provisional certificate, it is valid only for 5 years.

How does SSI/MSME registration help me get a loan from a bank?

All financial institutions, including the major banks, recognise MSMEs/SSIs and have devised special schemes for them. MSMEs and SSIs are earmarked for priority sector lending and can get loans at lower interests at select banks.

Is an Aadhaar card mandatory for MSME registration?

Yes, an Aadhaar card is mandatory for MSME registration.
