DSC

Digital Signature Certificates (DSC) are the digital equivalent of physical or paper certificates. Certificates serve as proof of identity of an individual for a certain purpose.Likewise, a digital certificate can be presented electronically to prove one’s identity, to access information or services on the Internet or to sign certain documents digitally.

Types of DSC

Class 1

  • Basic level of assurance
  • Used to verify user’s name and email id

    • Class 2

  • Issued for both business personnel and private individuals use
  • Issuance of Class 2 DSC is discontinued from 1st January 2021

    • Class 3

  • Thorough verification of user identity
  • It is issued to individuals and organisations that want to participate in bidding for government tenders or online tenders and auctions.

    Required Documents

    01

    Indians

    • ID proof: passport/driving license or any photo identity issued by the govt PAN card
    • Address proof: Aadhaar card/voter ID/driving license/registration certificate Attesting officer proof

    02

    Indian organizations

    • Organizational PAN
    • Partnership deed, if applicable
    • List of authorised signatories which could be the list of directors, board resolution, etc.
    • Authorised signatory ID proof
    • GST certificate

    03

    Foreign individuals and organizations

    • Attested copy of applicant’s passport
    • Attested copy of visa, if the applicant is out of their native country
    • Attested copy of resident permit certificate, if the applicant is in India
    • Attested copy of any govt-issued address proof

    Some common questions
    were often asked

    What is the validity period of a Digital Signature Certificate?

    The Certifying Authorities are authorized to issue a Digital Signature Certificate with a validity of one or two years.

    Is Director Identification Number (DIN) a pre-requisite to apply for DSC?

    No.

    What is the legal status of a Digital Signature?

    Digital Signatures are legally admissible in a Court of Law, as provided under the provisions of IT Act, 2000.

    What type of Digital Signature Certificate (DSC) is to be obtained for e-Filing on the MCA Portal?

    DSC of either Class 2 and Class 3 signing certificate category issued by a licensed Certifying Authority (CA) needs to be obtained for e-Filing on the MCA Portal.
